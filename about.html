About Me

Hello,

Once again, welcome to my website. Please enjoy my following mini-autobiography:

Here is a self-taken picture of me and the town in which I was born. I left this town, St. Gallen, Switzerland, at the age of six when I moved to the United States. Since then I lived in Ann Arbor, MI, for twelve years, and in Berkeley, CA, for the last five. Having experienced these semi-urban environments, with each consecutive location having a greater pollution problems, I’ve become interested in the social-environmental interface. While I originally wanted to express this interest through architecture, I’ve transitioned onto the Ph.D. route on which I am studying complex systems through data science!

I spent 2012 - 2016 completing a B.S. in the College of Natural Resources and a B.A. in the College of Environmental Design at the University of California, Berkeley. These degrees have provided me with the social and environmental science foundation that I now refer to when studying these fields in unison.

As mentioned earlier, I am now a graduate student. Specifically, I am working towards Ph.D. in Geography, Earth System Science (still at Berkeley) while concurrently taking part in a two-year National Science Foundation Data Science program and a Designated Emphasis in Computational and Data Science and Engineering.

As you may wonder, now that you know I am a geographer: yes, I do produce maps. Though I should note: these maps are rather complimentary to my research than my main product. I mainly focus on quantifying relationships within socio-enviornmental systems. Currently I’m looking at critical time scales of water storage in the Sierra Nevada and regime shifts in the Moorean coral reefs. Maps are used as a way to present the spatial data of my work.
Cheers!
