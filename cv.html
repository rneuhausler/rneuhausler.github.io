Research


Utilizing Remote Sensing techniques in Regime Shift Studies 08/2016 - present
Detecting environmental regime shifts in reefs from coral to algae dominated states with remote sensed data.
Applying concepts from information theory and complexity theory to determine main actors in costal Island socio-environmental systems.

Modeling for the Moorea Island Digital Ecosystem Avatar (Oxford, UK) 06/2016 - 07/2016
Contributing to the Moorea IDEA, a multi-university initiative striving to advance complex systems modeling at the socio-environmental interface.
In collaboration with Dr. Maria Bruna and Dr. Martin Robinson, spent five weeks over the summer at Oxford University creating a stochastic spatio-temporal model of coral cover as a function of various environmental inputs.

Salmon Creek Endmember Analysis: Methodological Comparison 10/2016 - 03/2017
Comparing stable isotopes and fluorescence in their ability to discriminate between different sources of water within a catchment. In collaboration with Prof. Laurel Larsen, continuing her initial research on the role of riparian zones in stream water through the combination of flourescence spectroscopy and end member mixing analysis.

Fluvial Geomorphology, Everglades Study 07/2014 - 05/2015

Manipulating a provided MATLAB code to perform noise reduction on flow rate data that was then used to determine bed shear stresses for calculating sediment transport.

Flood Inundation Simulations of the Bay Area and Sacramento Delta 03/2014 - 03/2015
Research assistant for John Radke, professor in Landscape Architecture Department, UC Berkeley
